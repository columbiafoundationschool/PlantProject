Arjuna

Introduction

The name Arjuna is mostly related to the warrior prince in Mahabharata but for those familiar with Ayurvedic medicine, it is also an effective herbal medicine.  Belonging to the Combretaceae family, with the Latin name is Terminalia Arjuna, this reddish bark of the plant is highly valued for its medicinal properties. In India, Arjuna is one of the most sacred trees, where the leaves and flowers are offered to Lord Vishnu and Lord Ganesha.

Side Effects Of Arjuna:

When taken in measured doses, arjuna bark powders and infused formulations are very safe for internal consumption and do not pose any health hazards. However, ingesting too much of this herbal powder, paste or distilled concoctions can lead to health issues in the stomach, liver and kidneys.

Benefits of arjuna:

  • Helps in controlling blood pressure.
  • Healthy reproductive tissues.
  • Comfortable digestion.
