Nahar

Introduction

Mesua ferrea Linn, also known as nahar in Hindi, and Ceylon ironwood in English is a tree with high medicinal and ornamental value. Mesua ferrea or nahar is a medium to a large-sized evergreen tree with a short trunk widely distributed in Kerala, Assam, Tamil NƒÅdu, Western, and the Eastern Ghats in India. The flowers and leaves of nahar are used for a variety of health conditions.

Distribution:

Plant is commonly distributed in mid-hills of Eastern Himalayas and rain forests of Konkan and Karnataka in Western Ghat.Plant is also distributed in Nepal eastwards, in north-eastern India, Deccan Peninsula and in the Andaman Islands.

Benefits of Nahar for Skin:

  • The kernels of nahar are helpful in skin eruption.
  • The oil obtained from nahar can be used for skin infections, scabies, and wounds.
  • nahar also contains antiseptic and disinfecting properties.
  • nahar taken with red sandalwood can be made into a paste and used on the skin to fade away the dark marks on the face and to obtain glowing skin.
